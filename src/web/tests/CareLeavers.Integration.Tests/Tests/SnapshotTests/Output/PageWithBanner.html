    Work and employment support

    Support and services to help with finding and staying in work

    Care leaver support

    Support you might get if you have been in care or ‘looked after’

    Planning your career

    Personal adviser (PA)

    Someone who helps you plan your career or find and stay in work

    Pathway Plan

    A plan you make with your PA that covers work and career goals, including support you need

    Finding work or training

    Apprenticeships Care Leavers’ Bursary (Opens in a new tab)

    Money to help you if you're doing an apprenticeship

    Care Leaver Covenant (Opens in a new tab)

    Opportunities and offers for care leavers, including jobs, apprenticeships and discounts

    Civil Service Care Leavers Internship Scheme (Opens in a new tab)

    Scheme offering care leavers an 18-month paid internship in UK government

    Local support

    You might get support from your local council’s Local Offer for care leavers

    For example, this could include things like:

    • help with travel costs (like a bus pass) or essential clothes for work or interviews
    • careers advice and guidance, such as help with job searches or writing your CV
    • work experience or an apprenticeship at your council

    Find out more about Local Offers

    Know what support you can get

    Use your 'care leaver status' to find out what support you have the right to.

    Check your care leaver status

    More support

    Support that your personal adviser can help you apply for:

    Access to Work (Opens in a new tab)

    To help get you into or stay in work if you have a physical or mental health condition or disability.

    Universal Credit (Opens in a new tab)

    A payment to help you with living costs if you’re on a low income, out of work or cannot work.

    Reasonable adjustment (Opens in a new tab)

    Adjustments employers must make for people with a disability, illness or health condition.

    Work and health programme (Opens in a new tab)

    Help finding and keeping a job if you’re out of work.
